Speech Recognition and Understanding in Trauma Resuscitation

Research area: Speech capture, noise reduction, speech recognition and understanding in trauma resuscitation room. Information extraction and natural language processing based on trauma transcripts.

Features

Speech analysis and recognition in the trauma room
we present a preliminary study on automatic speech recognition based on audio data from the trauma resuscitation room. We analyze the speech and environment of the trauma room by using the actual trauma resuscitation audio data observed in the trauma center, and explore the impact of factors of applying speech recognition in this scenario. The result shows that the trauma resuscitation room is a specific and complicated environment, which contains multiple noise, unbalanced speech distribution, semi-command speech structure, limited speech content, and a high speaking rate. To apply a speech recognition system in this environment, it is necessary to design a customized continuous distant-talking speech recognition system targeting team leader group members (Charge Nurse, Surgical Coordinator, and EM Attending) and the Jr. Resident.

 

Speech intent recognition
Speech provides text information and acoustic information for the speech recognition systems, however, most of the current systems interpret the intent of the sentence by the textual message and ignore the information from the acoustics. However, in real communications, people use pitch counter, stress pattern, and rhythm to express their intent not just by lexeme, especially in the trauma room. For example, trauma staffs often use intonation to express a question that the grammar of this sentence belongs to the statement. “Pain in belly” can be both a report to the trauma team or a question for the patient from physician by different intonation. Hence, how to combine the textual information and acoustic information together for the speech intent recognition is a necessary step for our research.

 

Sentence-level task classification and speech understanding
How to analyze the speech recognition result automatically and classify them by the trauma task dictionary are important for language understanding in the trauma room. Sentence-level based task classification is considered during the modeling. we propose a method to classify and analyze the speech recognition result, 28 tasks have been classified during the modeling and we are trying to collect more data for this research.

 

Speech recognition based on the Android tablet
Offline speech recognition system with android studio has been designed for the trauma room. We apply speech APIs provided by Pocketsphinx into the Android software, in general, the software enables translate speech to text and show it on screen in real time, record voice in background, label time stamp for each sentence, and store the translated sentences. We build our own dictionary and language model, which means we use the recoding typical sentences in hospital to train our system. 25 cases and ordinary medical sentences have been applied during the training. The test accuracy is almost 90% in quiet scenes.
